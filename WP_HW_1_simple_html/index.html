A short introduction to Airsoft

What is Airsoft?

Airsoft is a sport where you use airsoft guns, formally called replicas because of woke people, to shoot at your opponents. The replicas are made to look like real guns, but they shoot plastic BBs instead of bullets. They can be powered by batteries, gas, or springs.
Some are even powered by compressed air canisters strapped to your back.
The guns are usually made of plastic, but some more expensive are made of metal, for realism.
Most are made to resemble real firearms, although some original designs exist.
They are easy to distinguish from real guns, as they sound like toys.

How to play Airsoft?

Search on Google for your local Airsoft fields, and go for a few matches.
If you decide you like it, you can buy your own gear (in order of importance):
  1. Eye protection (most important)
  2. Replica
  3. BBs
  4. Face protection
  5. Magazines
  6. Ear protection
  7. Body protection
  8. Camouflage
  9. Hydration
  10. Communication
  11. Medical

Variations of Airsoft

There are many variations of Airsoft, including:

Casual
Played at small fields, participants are not required to wear certain camouflage types, and any type of replicas are allowed.
Speedsoft
Played with high ROF guns, and usually casual, comfortable clothes.
Milsim
Played with realistic guns, and usually with full body protection and realistic military gear.
